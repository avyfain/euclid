# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Curious general readers, students, and teachers reading Euclid closely. They need the original argument to remain primary while a geometric figure makes each construction easier to see.

## Product Purpose

Present Heath's translation of Euclid's *Elements* as a calm, searchable modern reader. Success means the text stays authoritative while navigation, commentary, and restrained visual explanation remove incidental friction.

## Brand Personality

Precise, calm, quietly playful. The reader should feel scholarly without becoming institutional and crafted without becoming precious.

## Anti-references

Avoid gamified classroom software, dense CAD controls, dashboard UI, generic textbook clip art, and interactions that ask the reader to learn a tool before learning the proposition.

## Design Principles

- Keep Euclid's text authoritative; additions clarify rather than paraphrase it away.
- Show constructions spatially and reveal only the step that matters.
- Make the first state useful, with one obvious interaction at most.
- Preserve the reader's editorial rhythm and historical texture.
- Prefer exact geometry and legible labels over decorative spectacle.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Support keyboard interaction and reduced motion, keep labels readable at narrow widths, and never make color the sole carrier of geometric meaning.
